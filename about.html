The Vision

We are building something that shouldn't require explanation — but somehow does.

What is a regenerative village?

It's a piece of land held in common — not owned by one person, but by a community of co-owners who commit to living in right relationship with it. The soil improves each year. The water is cleaner. The biodiversity grows richer. The people who live there make that possible, deliberately and together.

"Regenerative" is not a marketing word here. It is the operating principle. Every decision about the land — what gets planted, what gets built, how water flows — is made with the question: are we leaving this better than we found it?

Why co-ownership?

Individual ownership concentrates land in fewer and fewer hands. Investment vehicles extract value rather than create it. We want neither.

Co-ownership means the community holds the asset collectively. No single person can sell it from underneath everyone else. No investor can extract rent indefinitely. The founding co-owners establish the governance structure together — before land is acquired — so the terms are set by the people who will actually live by them.

This is not utopian fantasy. Land trusts, cooperatives, and commons-based governance have worked for centuries. We're building on those foundations with modern legal and digital tools.

Who is this for?

People who care deeply about nature and want to live near it — not visit it. People who value genuine community over curated lifestyle aesthetics. People who are ready to share in both the work and the governance of a place.

We are not building a resort, a co-working retreat, or a festival. We're building a home — for a community of good people who want to care for land and each other across decades.

Where are we now?

Early. We are forming the founding cohort before acquiring land — because the community should shape the criteria, not the other way around. We are looking for people who want to be part of the founding conversation, not just receive a finished product.

If that's you, join the waitlist. We'll be in touch when we're ready to bring founding co-owners into the process.
